i - Musical Artificial Intelligence

Will Artificial Intelligence replace musicians?

In some senses, yes. Computers are very good at large scale calculation. We can now get them to recognise pattern and, to a lesser extent, reproduce them through computational means.

If a computer can create a disembodied version of a musician and create music in their style (see multiple attempts on recreation of the works of Bach) does this, haven't we in some senses replaced the musician?

Replacing musicians with computers forces us to evaluate what it means to create music in the first place.

Artificial intelligence won't (most likely) replace musicians outright in the near future. Sentient AI is still mostly only observed in science fiction, although science fiction often shapes our technological landscapes.

Pushing the sentient narrative is often a political choice to divert people's attention from current technological landscapes and the economical meanings of this.

Aritificial Intelligence is another technology, as computers, transducers, electricity were before it. The goal of technology should be to help automate processes and allow being human to become easier.
